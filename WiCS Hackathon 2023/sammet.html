Jean E. Sammet




Wrote the first widely used computer program for FORMAC, a symbolic manipulation of mathematical formulas. After this big breakthrough, Sammet went to pursue more direct work with computers by joining Sylvania. She and five other programmers created COBOL, another CS language that is still utilized today. Additionally, Sammet became the programming language technology manager for IBM and also led the development of the Ada programming language by Augusta Ada Lovelace.
